## Spotted Lantern Flytrap

**Team:** Batties  
**Clients:** Cornell CALS Extension, E&J Gallo Winery, National Grape

---

### Problem Statement
Up to **400 spotted lanternflies (SLF)** can feed on each vine in a vineyard, and over time this repetitive feeding can cause **up to 90% yield loss**.

---

### Impact
As long as SLF continue feeding on grape vines, grapes are less likely to survive to harvest. By focusing specifically on vineyards, we can develop a targeted, industry-specific solution.

---

### Proposed Direction

**Concept:** *Spotted Lantern Flytrap*

**What it is:**  
A trap that attracts SLF with bait and captures them using one of several mechanisms:
- Sticky adhesive surface capture
- Low-power fan containment system
- Soap-water reservoir trap

**How it would be used**
- Hung on trees near vineyards  
- Checked and cleared periodically  

**Why it’s better than current methods**
- SLF populations are too large for manual removal  
- Reduces risk of later crop contamination  

---

### End-of-Semester Proof of Concept
We will build a **single one-way entrance chamber** containing bait to test whether SLF can be reliably attracted and captured using passive bait combined with mechanical containment.

---

### Key Risks & Unknowns
- **Capturing pollinators:** Many are endangered. We will test bait outdoors to see what species are attracted.
- **SLF ignoring bait:** If they prefer grapes, traps may fail. We will compare attraction to different baits, including grape vines.
- **SLF escaping trap:** We will test prototypes using live SLF and SLF-sized objects.

---

### Questions for the Client
1. Are there substances more attractive to SLF than grape vines? Are they drawn to scent, sight, or another stimulus?  
   *Decision impact:* Determines optimal attraction mechanism.

2. Which other insects are attracted to the same stimuli as SLF?  
   *Decision impact:* Helps select bait that minimizes bycatch.

3. What size/weight are SLF when they enter vineyards?  
   *Decision impact:* Ensures proper mesh and trap dimensions.
